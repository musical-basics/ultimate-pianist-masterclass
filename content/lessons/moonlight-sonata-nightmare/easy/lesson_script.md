# Moonlight Sonata Nightmare — Easy Version
## Lesson Script

**Target length:** 20–30 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Beginner–Intermediate  
**Prerequisites:** Can play hands separately, basic chord knowledge

---

## Before You Film

- Sit at the piano with the arrangement propped up or on an iPad beside you
- Have the full Nightmare version open too so you can reference it
- Talk like you're teaching a single student in a private lesson — not a lecture
- Don't memorize this. Use the beats as a scaffold and teach naturally

---

## Beat 1: The Welcome + What This Is (2–3 min)

- "Welcome to the first lesson inside The Ultimate Pianist. I'm so glad you're here."
- "Today we're starting with one of my most popular pieces — Moonlight Sonata Nightmare."
- "If you've heard the original Beethoven, you know how hauntingly beautiful it is. My arrangement — the Nightmare version — takes that and pushes it into this dark, cinematic place."
- "But here's the thing: a lot of people who bought it told me they couldn't get through it. The jumps are wide, the left hand is relentless, and the tempo is unforgiving."
- "So today, we're starting with the Easy version. This is a playable, beautiful arrangement that captures the soul of the original — without the parts that will destroy your hands."
- "By the end of this lesson, you'll understand exactly how this piece is structured, and you'll have a practice roadmap to actually learn it."

---

## Beat 2: Big Picture — How the Piece is Built (3–5 min)

Walk through the arrangement at a high level. Don't teach note by note yet — just orient them.

- **Key:** C# minor (same as Beethoven's original)
- **Time signature:** 4/4 simplified (vs the original 6/8 feel)
- **Structure:**
  - Intro (bars 1–4): Establish the mood. Slow, sparse, haunting.
  - Main theme (bars 5–20): The iconic melody in the right hand, steady accompaniment in the left.
  - Build section (bars 21–32): More movement, slight increase in energy.
  - Return + Outro (bars 33–end): The melody comes back, then fades.

- "The good news? The easy version uses the same chord progression as the full Nightmare. So once you learn this, you already understand the harmonic DNA of the whole arrangement."

---

## Beat 3: The Left Hand — The Foundation (5–8 min)

Teach the left hand pattern first. This is the key to making the piece feel right.

- **Pattern:** A broken chord / arpeggiated pattern (e.g., root → 5th → octave or root → 3rd → 5th depending on your arrangement)
- Demonstrate slowly, then at tempo
- "The left hand is your heartbeat. Once it feels automatic, everything else gets easier."
- Common mistake: rushing the pattern. "It's meditative. Think of it like a slow tide rolling in."
- **Practice tip:** Loop just bars 1–8, left hand only, for 5 minutes before touching the right hand.

---

## Beat 4: The Right Hand — The Melody (5–8 min)

Now teach the melody.

- "This is the part everyone recognizes. Your job is to make it sing."
- Demonstrate the opening phrase
- Focus on:
  - **Finger legato:** connect each note like you're drawing a line
  - **Dynamics:** start soft (pp), let it swell naturally
  - **Timing:** don't rush to the next note. Let each one breathe.
- "One of my favorite tricks: slow it down to 60% tempo and exaggerate the dynamics. If it sounds emotional at 60%, it'll sound incredible at full speed."

---

## Beat 5: Hands Together — The Tricky Moments (5–7 min)

Bring the hands together and flag the 2–3 spots where students will struggle.

**Spot 1: The first transition (e.g., bar 8 → 9)**
- The left hand shifts position while the right hand holds a melody note
- "Don't let your right hand choke the note while your left moves. Keep the melody alive, then move the bass."

**Spot 2: The build section**
- More notes, slightly faster movement
- "If this section feels chaotic, isolate it. Just bars 21–28, hands together, 10 times in a row. Slow."

**Spot 3: The final return**
- The melody repeats but with a slightly different ending — don't auto-pilot
- "This is where you decide how the piece ends. Add a little ritardando. Let it breathe."

---

## Beat 6: The Mindset Reminder (1–2 min)

- "Learning a piece like this is not about grinding through it until your hands give up."
- "It's about slow reps, staying relaxed, and trusting that muscle memory is being built every time you play it right."
- "One week of 15-minute daily practice will get you further than a 2-hour marathon session on a weekend."
- "I want to hear you play this. Send me a video when you get the first 8 bars down."

---

## Beat 7: What's Next (30 sec)

- "Once you have the Easy version down, the Medium version lesson is waiting for you. That's where we start adding some of the signature Nightmare elements — the bass runs, the dynamic swells, the extended range."
- "I'll see you in the next lesson."

---

## Notes for Filming

- Keep a glass of water nearby — you'll be demonstrating a lot
- Film with two angles if possible: wide shot of full body at piano, and close-up of hands
- Don't be afraid to play mistakes on camera and correct them — it's humanizing
- Each "Beat" can be a chapter marker in the final edit
